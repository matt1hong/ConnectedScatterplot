Study on Connected Scatterplots and Dual-Axis Line Charts

In this study, you will be asked to translate data points from one type of chart into another. Please read the instructions below carefully before moving on.

The two types of chart used are dual-axis line charts and connected scatterplots. There will be four phases in this study. In two of them, you will be copying from one chart to an identical one, in the two others you will be translating between the two different types.

Both charts show data over time. Dual-axis line charts have a time axis along the bottom, and two vertical value axes. The points in the chart, as well as the lines between them, depict the data. The two axis on the left and right are color-coded to match the colors of the lines they correspond to.

Connected scatterplots show the data in a two-dimensional space, where one value axis is horizontal, the other one vertical. The points represent points in time, just like in dual-axis line charts. In addition to the lines, the connected scatterplots shows arrows to indicate direction. The colors on the axis are the same as in the dual-axis line chart, and both represent the same data.
